    üîó
    • Integrating both sides with respect to \(x\text{.}\)
    • Incorrect. Integration should come after the equation is verified to be separable.
    • Check to make sure the equation is first order.
    • Correct! The first step to verify the equation is separable is to make sure it is first order.
    • Combine the fractions on the left-side of the equation.
    • Incorrect. First of all, \(\frac{dy}{dx}\) is not a fraction. Even if it was this would not be the first step to verify separability.
    • Isolate \(\ds\frac{dy}{dx}\) on one side of the equation.
    • Incorrect. Isolating \(\ds\frac{dy}{dx}\) is required to verify the equation is separable, but there is one other detail to check first.
