✳️ Solving Quadratic Equations.

The solution to the quadratic equation
\begin{equation} a x^2 + b x + c = 0\tag{46} \end{equation}
is given by the quadratic formula:
\begin{equation} x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{\color{blue} b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}\text{.}\tag{47} \end{equation}
Notes:
  1. The \(\pm\) gives two solutions, say \(x_1\) and \(x_2\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
  2. \(x_1\) and \(x_2\) are also known as the roots of \(\ a x^2 + b x + c\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
  3. The value, \({\color{blue} b^2 - 4ac} \ \text{,}\) under the root in is called the discriminant.
    🔗
    🔗
  4. Equation (46) can be written as \(\quad\ds (x - x_1)(x - x_2) = 0 \text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
  5. If \({\color{blue} b^2 - 4ac > 0}\text{,}\) then \(x_1\) and \(x_2\) are different real numbers.
    🔗
    🔗
  6. If \({\color{blue} b^2 - 4ac = 0}\text{,}\) then \(x_1\) and \(x_2\) are the same real number (repeated).
    🔗
    🔗
  7. If \({\color{blue} b^2 - 4ac < 0}\text{,}\) then \(x_1\) and \(x_2\) are complex and can be written as
    \begin{equation*} x_1 = \alpha + \beta i, \quad x_2 = \alpha - \beta i\text{.} \end{equation*}
    🔗
    🔗
