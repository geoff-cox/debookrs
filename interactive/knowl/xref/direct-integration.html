Method 1. Direct Integration.

The general solution of a differential equation in the form
\begin{equation} \frac{d}{dx}\left[g(x,y)\right] = f(x)\text{,}\tag{1} \end{equation}
can be determined by through the following steps:
Integrate both Sides
Integrate both sides with respect to the independent variable, \(x\text{,}\) to eliminate the derivative containing \(y\text{.}\)
Isolate the Solution
Now, isolate the dependent variable, \(y\text{,}\) to find the general solution.
