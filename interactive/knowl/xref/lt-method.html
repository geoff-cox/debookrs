✍🏻 Method 6. Laplace Transform Method (Three-Step Roadmap).

Given an \(n\)-th-order IVP
\begin{equation*} y^{(n)} + a_{n-1}y^{(n-1)} + \dots + a_1y' + a_0y = g(t), \end{equation*}
the particular solution is obtained through the following workflow:
🔗
Step 1— Forward Transform
🔗
Apply \(\laplacesym\) term-by-term. Powers of \(s\) replace derivatives, and the initial conditions drop in automatically. The result is a single algebraic (Laplace-domain) equation.
🔗
Step 2— Solve & Prepare \(Y(s)\)
🔗
Isolate \(Y(s)\text{,}\) then reshape it so every term matches an entry in the transform table. This may require tools like partial-fraction decomposition and completing the square.
🔗
Step 3— Inverse Transform
🔗
Apply \(\laplacesym^{-1}\) term-by-term. The hidden original-domain solution \(y(t)\) is released, and the IVP is solved.
