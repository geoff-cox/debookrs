✍🏻 Method 2. Steps for Using Separation of Variables.

Step 1: Verify Separability
🔗
(i) Check that your equation is first-order and (ii) can be rearranged into the form
\begin{equation*} \frac{dy}{dx} = f(x) \cdot g(y). \end{equation*}
🔗
Step 2: Separate and Integrate
🔗
(i) Rearrange so that all \(y\) terms are on one side (with \(dy\)) and \(x\) terms are on the other (with \(dx\)), then (ii) integrate:
🔗
\begin{equation*} \int \frac{1}{g(y)}\, dy = \int f(x)\, dx. \end{equation*}
(iii) Compute the integrals on both sides.
🔗
Step 3: Solve for \(y\)
🔗
If possible, explicitly solve the resulting equation for \(y\text{.}\) If not, leave the solution in implicit form. Remember to combine integration constants when appropriate.
