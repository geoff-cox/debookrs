Method 2. Separation of Variables.

If a differential equation can be written in the separable form
\begin{equation*} \frac{dy}{dx} = f(x) \cdot g(y)\text{,} \end{equation*}
then the general solution can be determined through the steps below.
Step 1: Separate
\(\displaystyle \displaystyle \frac{1}{g(y)}\ dy = f(x)\ dx\)
Step 2: Integrate
\(\displaystyle \displaystyle \int \frac{1}{g(y)}\ dy = \int f(x) \ dx\)
Step 3: Finalize
Isolate \(y\) (if possible), and combine constants.
