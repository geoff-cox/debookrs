\(\lap{t^2}\) Details.

Using the definition of the Laplace transform:
\begin{equation*} \lap{ t^2 } = \lim_{b \to \infty} \ub{\int_0^b e^{-st} \cdot t^2\ dt}_{I} \end{equation*}
Integration by parts on \(I\), gives us
\begin{align*} \phantom{\lap{t}} =\amp\ \lim_{b \to \infty} \left[-\frac{b^2}{s}e^{-sb} + \frac{2}{s}\int_0^b e^{-st}\cdot t\ dt\right]\\ \phantom{\lap{t}} =\amp\ -\frac{1}{s} \lim_{b \to \infty} \left[-\frac{b^2}{e^{sb}}\right] + \frac{2}{s}\lim_{b \to \infty}\int_0^b e^{-st}\cdot t\ dt\\ \phantom{\lap{t}} =\amp\ -\frac{1}{s}\ub{\lim_{b \to \infty}\frac{b^2}{e^{sb}}}_{L} + \frac{2}{s}\ub{\int_0^\infty e^{-st}\cdot t\ dt}_{\lap{t}} \end{align*}
L’Hopital’s Rule shows \(L=0\) and \(\lap{t}=1/s^2\text{.}\) Therefore,
\begin{equation*} \lap{ t^2 } = \frac{2}{s^3}, \quad s > 0\text{.} \end{equation*}
