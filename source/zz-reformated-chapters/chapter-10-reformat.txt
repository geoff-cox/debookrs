<METADATA>
  <title> Laplace Transform Method </title>
  <label>chap-laplace-method</label>
  <overview>Introduces the Laplace transform method for solving initial value problems by converting differential equations into algebraic equations, solving for Y(s), and returning to y(t).</overview>
  <dependencies>Requires familiarity with the Laplace transform definition, properties, and the table of common transforms.</dependencies>
</METADATA>

<overview>
  <p>Imagine turning a complex differential equation into an algebra problem instead. That is the magic of the Laplace transform method. It transforms derivatives into powers of s and automatically incorporates initial conditions as constants. This chapter walks through the method in three steps: forward transform, algebra in the Laplace domain, and inverse transform.</p>
</overview>

<objectives>
  <ul>
    <li>Understand the three-step Laplace transform method.</li>
    <li>Apply the Laplace transform to initial value problems.</li>
    <li>Use algebraic manipulation to solve for Y(s).</li>
    <li>Prepare Y(s) for inversion using partial fractions and completing the square.</li>
    <li>Apply the inverse Laplace transform to find y(t).</li>
  </ul>
</objectives>

<core-narrative>

  <section label="lt-method-step1">
    <title> Step 1 — Into the Laplace Domain </title>
    <overview>Convert a differential equation into an algebraic equation in the Laplace domain by applying the Laplace transform to both sides, replacing derivatives with powers of s.</overview>

    <examples>
      <example role="forward-transform-demo">
        <title> Forward Transforming Equations </title>
        <statement>Forward transform the differential equation y'' - 4y' + 6y = t^2, y(0) = 2, y'(0) = 0 into the Laplace domain.</statement>
      </example>
    </examples>

    <section-summary>
      <ul>
        <li>Applying the Laplace transform converts each derivative into powers of s and drops in initial conditions automatically.</li>
        <li>The unknown function y(t) is now stored inside Y(s).</li>
      </ul>
    </section-summary>
  </section>

  <section label="lt-method-step2">
    <title> Step 2 — Solving in the Laplace Domain </title>
    <overview>After transforming the equation, solve for Y(s) algebraically, then rewrite it to match known inverse Laplace transform forms.</overview>

    <examples>
      <example role="laplace-domain-solve">
        <title> Solving a Laplace-Domain Equation </title>
        <statement>Solve for Y(s) in s^2 Y - 2s - 4sY + 5 + 6Y = 2/s^3.</statement>
      </example>
      <example role="partial-fraction">
        <title> Partial Fraction Decomposition </title>
        <statement>Y(s) = (2s + 5)/(s^2 + 5s + 4)</statement>
      </example>
    </examples>

    <section-summary>
      <ul>
        <li>Isolate Y(s) using algebra.</li>
        <li>Use partial fractions and completing the square to rewrite Y(s) into invertible pieces.</li>
      </ul>
    </section-summary>
  </section>

  <section label="lt-method-step3">
    <title> Step 3 — Leaving the Laplace Domain </title>
    <overview>Return to the original time domain by applying the inverse Laplace transform to Y(s), recovering y(t).</overview>

    <examples>
      <example role="inverse-transform-table">
        <title> Using the Table for Inverse Laplace </title>
        <statement>Invert Y(s) = 3/(s^2 + 9).</statement>
      </example>
    </examples>

    <section-summary>
      <ul>
        <li>Apply the inverse Laplace transform term-by-term to Y(s).</li>
        <li>Match each piece to the transform table and reconstruct y(t).</li>
      </ul>
    </section-summary>
  </section>

  <section label="lt-method-steps-examples">
    <title> Summary & Worked Examples </title>
    <overview>All three steps together: forward transform, solve and prepare Y(s), and inverse transform to recover y(t).</overview>

    <examples>
      <example role="full-method-example">
        <title> Basic Second-Order, Homogeneous </title>
        <statement>Solve y'' + 5y' + 6y = 0, y(0) = 2, y'(0) = -1 using the Laplace transform method.</statement>
      </example>
      <example role="full-method-example">
        <title> Nonhomogeneous Forcing Function </title>
        <statement>Solve y'' - 2y' + y = e^{t}, y(0) = 0, y'(0) = 1 using the Laplace transform method.</statement>
      </example>
    </examples>

    <section-summary>
      <ul>
        <li>The three-step Laplace roadmap always applies: forward transform → solve for Y(s) → inverse transform.</li>
        <li>Algebra in Step 2 is where the main variety occurs.</li>
      </ul>
    </section-summary>
  </section>

</core-narrative>
