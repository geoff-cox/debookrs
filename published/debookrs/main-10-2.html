    Section B.1 Laplace Transforms

    Notice that when \(s=0\text{,}\) the term in the integral becomes \(\ds e^{0t} = 1\) and we, instead, would get
    \begin{align*} =\amp\ 15 \lim_{b \to \infty}\int_0^b 1\ dt = 15 \lim_{b \to \infty} t\Big|_0^b = 15 \lim_{b \to \infty} b = \infty. \end{align*}
    This shows that when \(s=0\text{,}\) the integral does not converge (does not exist) as required by the definition of the Laplace Transform. Therefore, when working with Laplace transforms, it is common restrict some values of \(s\text{.}\)
    When \(s=0\text{,}\) the the integral becomes
    \begin{align*} =\amp\ \lim_{b \to \infty} \int_0^b 11t\ dt = \lim_{b \to \infty} \frac{11t^2}{2}\Big|_0^b = \frac{11}{2} \lim_{b \to \infty} b^2 = \infty. \end{align*}
    Therefore, we must have \(s\ne0\) for this integral to be finite.
    If \(s \lt 0\text{,}\) then as \(b \to \infty\text{,}\) we would have \(\ds e^{-sb} \to \infty\) and so
    \begin{equation*} \lim_{b \to \infty} \os{\infty}{\os{\uparrow}{\boxed{b}}}\ \us{\infty}{\us{\downarrow}{\boxed{e^{-sb}}}} = \infty. \end{equation*}
    This shows the Laplace transform would not exist if \(s \lt 0\text{.}\) Therefore, we must require \(s \gt 0\text{.}\)
    Since the limit only controls \(b\text{,}\) we treat \(s\) as a constant, hence \(\ds-\frac{1}{s}\) is also a constant that can be pulled out of the limit.
    Since \(b \to \infty\text{,}\) then \(\os{negative}{\ob{(7-s)}}b \to -\infty\) and therefore, \(\ds e^{\!\!\os{\Large negative}{\ob{(7-s)}}\!\!b} \to 0\)
    We’ve seen the second limit, but let’s review it again. If \(s \gt 0\text{,}\) then \(sb \to \infty\) and
    \begin{equation*} \lim_{b \to \infty} e^{-sb} = \lim_{b \to \infty} \frac{1}{\us{\infty}{\us{\downarrow}{\boxed{e^{sb}}}}} = 0 \end{equation*}
    The first limit requires L’Hopital’s rule. Remember, when using L’Hopital’s rule here, we must take derivatives with respect to \(b\) (where \(s\) is constant). Here are the details:
    \begin{align*} \lim_{b \to \infty}be^{-sb} =\amp\ \lim_{b \to \infty}\frac{\os{\infty}{\os{\uparrow}{\boxed{b}}}}{\us{\infty}{\us{\downarrow}{\boxed{e^{sb}}}}} \,\us{LH}{=}\, \lim_{b \to \infty}\frac{1}{se^{sb}} = \frac{1}{s}\lim_{b \to \infty}\frac{1}{\us{\infty}{\us{\downarrow}{\boxed{e^{sb}}}}} = 0, \quad s \gt 0 \end{align*}
    \begin{align*} u = t,\quad\amp dv = e^{-st}dt, \\ du = dt,\quad\amp v = -\frac{1}{s}e^{-st} \end{align*}
    \begin{align*} \int_0^b te^{-st} dt =\amp\ \left[t \cdot \left( -\frac{1}{s}e^{-st} \right) - \int \left( -\frac{1}{s}e^{-st} \right) dt\right]\Bigg|_0^b \\ =\amp\ \left[-\frac{t}{s}e^{-st} + \frac{1}{s}\int e^{-st} dt\right]\Bigg|_0^b \\ =\amp\ \left[-\frac{t}{s}e^{-st} - \frac{1}{s^2}e^{-st}\right]\Bigg|_0^b\\ =\amp\ \left[\left(-\frac{b}{s}e^{-sb} - \frac{1}{s^2}e^{-sb}\right) - \left(-\frac{0}{s^2}e^{-s\cdot 0} - \frac{1}{s^2}e^{-s\cdot 0}\right)\right]\\ =\amp\ \left[-\frac{b}{s}e^{-sb} - \frac{1}{s^2}e^{-sb} + \frac{1}{s^2}\right] \end{align*}
    Key Steps
    \begin{equation*} \begin{array}{ll} (1) \amp \text{IBP with }\\ \amp u = t^2, dv = e^{-st} dt\\ (2) \amp \text{factor out } -\frac{1}{s}\\ (3) \amp \text{definition of the}\\ \amp \text{Laplace transform of } t\\ (4) \amp \text{convert evaluation at } \infty\\ \amp \text{to the limit at } \infty \\ (5) \amp \text{L'Hopital's Rule }\\ \end{array} \end{equation*}
    \begin{align*} \lap{ t^2 } =\amp\ \int_0^{\infty} e^{-st}\cdot t^2 dt \\ \amp\os{(1)}{=} t^2 \cdot -\frac{1}{s}e^{-st}\Bigg|_0^{\infty} - \int_0^{\infty} -\frac{1}{s}e^{-st}\cdot 2tdt \\ \amp\os{(2)}{=} -\frac{1}{s}\left[ t^2e^{-st}\Bigg|_0^{\infty} - 2\int_0^{\infty}e^{-st}\cdot tdt \right] \\ \amp\os{(3)}{=} -\frac{1}{s}\left[t^2e^{-st}\Bigg|_0^{\infty} - 2\lap{ t } \right] \\ \amp\os{(4)}{=} -\frac{1}{s}\left[ \lim_{b \to \infty} t^2e^{-st}\Bigg|_0^{b} - 2\lap{ t } \right] \\ =\amp\ -\frac{1}{s}\left[ \lim_{b \to \infty} \left( b^2e^{-sb} - 0^2\cdot e^{-s\cdot 0} \right) - 2\cdot \frac{1}{s^2} \right] \\ =\amp\ -\frac{1}{s}\left[ \lim_{b \to \infty} b^2e^{-sb} - 0 - \frac{2}{s^2} \right] \\ =\amp\ -\frac{1}{s}\left[ \lim_{b \to \infty} \frac{b^2}{e^{sb}} - \frac{2}{s^2} \right] \\ \amp\os{(5)}{=} -\frac{1}{s}\left[ \lim_{b \to \infty} \frac{2b}{se^{sb}} - \frac{2}{s^2} \right] \\ =\amp\ -\frac{1}{s}\left[ \frac{2}{s}\lim_{b \to \infty} \frac{b}{e^{sb}} - \frac{2}{s^2} \right] \\ =\amp\ -\frac{1}{s}\left[ \frac{2}{s}\lim_{b \to \infty} \frac{1}{se^{sb}} - \frac{2}{s^2} \right] \\ =\amp\ -\frac{1}{s}\left[ \frac{2}{s}\cdot 0 - \frac{2}{s^2} \right] \\ =\amp\ \frac{2}{s^3} \end{align*}
    Key Steps
    \begin{equation*} \begin{array}{ll} (1) \amp \text{IBP with }\\ \amp u = t^3, dv = e^{-st} dt\\ (2) \amp \text{factor out } -\frac{1}{s}\\ (3) \amp \text{definition of the}\\ \amp \text{Laplace transform of } t\\ (4) \amp \text{convert evaluation at } \infty\\ \amp \text{to the limit at } \infty \\ (5) \amp \text{L'Hopital's Rule }\\ \end{array} \end{equation*}
    \begin{align*} \lap{ t^3 } =\amp\ \int_0^{\infty} e^{-st}\cdot t^3 dt \\ \amp\os{(1)}{=} t^3 \cdot -\frac{1}{s}e^{-st}\Bigg|_0^{\infty} - \int_0^{\infty} -\frac{1}{s}e^{-st}\cdot 3t^2dt \\ \amp\os{(2)}{=} -\frac{1}{s}\left[ t^3e^{-st}\Bigg|_0^{\infty} - 3\int_0^{\infty}e^{-st}\cdot t^2 dt \right]\\ \amp\os{(3)}{=} -\frac{1}{s}\left[ \lim_{b \to \infty}t^3 e^{-st}\Bigg|_0^{b} - 3\lap{ t^2 } \right]\\ \amp\os{(4)}{=} -\frac{1}{s}\left[ \lim_{b \to \infty}\left( b^3 e^{-sb} - 0^3\cdot e^{-s\cdot 0} \right) - 3\cdot \frac{2}{s^3} \right]\\ =\amp\ -\frac{1}{s}\left[ \lim_{b \to \infty}b^3 e^{-sb} - 0 - \frac{6}{s^3} \right]\\ =\amp\ -\frac{1}{s}\left[ \lim_{b \to \infty}\frac{b^3}{e^{sb}} - \frac{6}{s^3} \right]\\ \amp\os{(5)}{=} -\frac{1}{s}\left[ \lim_{b \to \infty}\frac{3b^2}{se^{sb}} - \frac{6}{s^3} \right]\\ =\amp\ -\frac{1}{s}\left[ \frac{3}{s}\lim_{b \to \infty}\frac{b^2}{e^{sb}} - \frac{6}{s^3} \right]\\ =\amp\ -\frac{1}{s}\left[ \frac{3}{s}\lim_{b \to \infty}\frac{2b}{se^{sb}} - \frac{6}{s^3} \right]\\ =\amp\ -\frac{1}{s}\left[ \frac{6}{s^2}\lim_{b \to \infty}\frac{b}{e^{sb}} - \frac{6}{s^3} \right]\\ =\amp\ -\frac{1}{s}\left[ \frac{6}{s^2}\lim_{b \to \infty}\frac{1}{se^{sb}} - \frac{6}{s^3} \right]\\ =\amp\ -\frac{1}{s}\left[ \frac{6}{s^2}\cdot 0 - \frac{6}{s^3} \right]\\ =\amp\ \frac{6}{s^4} \end{align*}
    Key Steps
    \begin{equation*} \begin{array}{ll} (1) \amp \text{IBP with }\\ \amp u = t^4, dv = e^{-st} dt\\ (2) \amp \text{factor out } -\frac{1}{s}\\ (3) \amp \text{definition of the}\\ \amp \text{Laplace transform of } t\\ (4) \amp \text{convert evaluation at } \infty\\ \amp \text{to the limit at } \infty \\ (5) \amp \text{L'Hopital's Rule }\\ \end{array} \end{equation*}
