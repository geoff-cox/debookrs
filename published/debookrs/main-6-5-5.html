    Subsection 4.3.2 Summary & Exercises

    Summary of the Key Ideas.

    Method

    Exercises Exercises

    .

    1.
    Use Laplace transforms to find the solutio \(\ds y(t) \) of the following initial value problem.\(\ds x'' + 6x' + 9x = 64e^{5t}, \hspace{0.5cm}x(0) = 3, \hspace{0.5cm} x'(0) = 0 \)
    Answer.
    \(\ds x = e^{5t} + 2e^{-3t} + te^{-3t} \)
    Solution.
    We begin by taking the Laplace transform and substituting in the initial conditions.
    \begin{align*} \Big[ s^2X(s) - sx(0) - x'(0) \Big] +6\Big[ sX(s) - x(0) \Big] + 9X(s) \amp = \frac{64}{s-5} \mbox{ (by L12, L11 and L2)} \\ \Big[ s^2X(s) - s\cdot 3 - 0 \Big] +6\Big[ sX(s) - 3 \Big] + 9X(s) \amp = \frac{64}{s-5} \\ s^2X(s) - 3s + 6sX(s) - 18 + 9X(s) \amp = \frac{64}{s-5} \\ s^2X(s) + 6sX(s)+9X(s) -3s - 18 \amp = \frac{64}{s-5} \end{align*}
    Now we aim to solve fo \(\ds X(s), \) so we use algebra to rearrange as follows.
    \begin{align*} s^2X(s) + 6sX(s)+9X(s) \amp = \frac{64}{s-5} + 3s + 18 \\ X(s) \amp = \frac{64}{s-5} + (3s + 18)\cdot \frac{s-5}{s-5} \\ \amp = \frac{64}{s-5} + \frac{(3s+18)(s-5)}{s-5} \\ \amp = \frac{64}{s-5} + \frac{3s^2 +3s- 90}{s-5} \\ \amp = \frac{64 + 3s^2 +3s- 90}{s-5} \\ \amp = \frac{3s^2 +3s- 26}{s-5} \\ X(s) \amp = \frac{3s^2 +3s- 26}{(s-5)(s^2 + 6s + 9)} \\ \amp = \frac{3s^2 +3s- 26}{(s-5)(s+3)^2} \end{align*}
    We need to do a partial fraction decomposition before we can take the inverse LT.
    \begin{align*} \frac{3s^2 +3s- 26}{(s-5)(s+3)^2} \amp = \frac{A}{s-5} + \frac{B}{s+3} + \frac{C}{(s+3)^2} \\ 3s^2 +3s - 26 \amp = A(s+3)^2 + B(s-5)(s+3) + C(s-5) \\ \ \amp = A(s^2 + 6s + 9) + B(s^2 - 2s - 15) + Cs - 5C \\ \amp = As^2 + 6As + 9A + Bs^2 - 2Bs - 15B + Cs - 5C \\ \amp = (A + B)s^2 + (6A - 2B + C)s + (9A - 15B - 5C) \end{align*}
    Equating the coefficients of like terms yields, we can solve fo \(\ds A \) \(\ds B \) , an \(\ds C \) .
    \begin{align*} A+B \amp = 3 \amp 6A - 2B + C \amp= 3 \amp 9A - 15B - 5C \amp= -26 \\ B \amp = 3-A \amp \amp \amp \amp \\ \amp \amp6A - 2(3-A)+C \amp = 3 \amp \amp \\ \amp \amp 6A-6+2A+C \amp = 3 \amp \amp \\ \amp \amp 8A + C \amp = 9 \amp \amp \\ \amp \amp C \amp = 9 - 8A \amp \amp \\ \amp \amp \amp \amp 9A - 15(3-A) - 5(9-8A) \amp = -26 \\ \amp \amp \amp \amp 9A - 45 + 15A - 45 + 40A \amp = -26 \\ \amp \amp \amp \amp 64A \amp = 64 \\ \amp \amp \amp \amp A \amp = 1 \\ \amp \amp C \amp = 9-8(1) \amp \amp \\ \amp \amp \amp = 1 \amp \amp \\ B \amp = 3 - 1 \amp \amp \amp \amp \\ \amp = 2 \amp \amp \amp \amp \end{align*}
    Hence we have \(\ds X(s) = \frac{1}{s-5} + \frac{2}{s+3} + \frac{1}{(s+3)^2}. \) Now we need only take the inverse LT to find the solution.
    \begin{align*} x(t) \amp = \lap^{-1}\left\{ \frac{1}{s-5} + \frac{2}{s+3} + \frac{1}{(s+3)^2} \right\} \\ \amp = \lap^{-1}\left\{ \frac{1}{s-5} \right\} + 2\cdot \lap^{-1}\left\{\frac{1}{s+3} \right\} + \lap^{-1}\left\{\frac{1}{(s+3)^2} \right\} \\ \amp = e^{5t} + 2e^{-3t} + te^{-3t} \end{align*}

    2.
    Use Laplace transforms to find the solutio \(\ds y(t) \) of the following initial value problem.\(\ds y''-2y'+5y = -8e^{-t}, \hspace{0.5cm}y(0) = 2, \hspace{0.5cm} y'(0) = 12 \)
    Answer.
    \(\ds y(t) = -e^{-t} + 3e^t \cos(2t) + 4e^t \sin(2t) \)
    Solution.
    We begin by taking the Laplace transform and substituting in the initial conditions.
    \begin{align*} \Big[ s^2Y(s) - sy(0) - y'(0) \Big] - 2\Big[ sY(s) - y(0) \Big] + 5Y(s) \amp = \frac{-8}{s+1} \mbox{ (by L12, L11 and L2)} \\ \Big[ s^2Y(s) - s\cdot 2 - 12) \Big] - 2\Big[ sY(s) - 2 \Big] + 5Y(s) \amp = \frac{-8}{s+1} \\ s^2Y(s) - 2s - 12 - 2sY(s) +4 + 5Y(s) \amp = \frac{-8}{s+1} \\ s^2Y(s) - 2sY(s) + 5Y(s)- 2s - 8 \amp = \frac{-8}{s+1} \end{align*}
    Now we aim to solve fo \(\ds Y(s), \) so we use algebra to rearrange as follows.
    \begin{align*} s^2Y(s) - 2sY(s) + 5Y(s) \amp = \frac{-8}{s+1} + 2s - 8 \\ (s^2 - 2s + 5)Y(s) \amp = \frac{-8}{s+1} + (2s - 8)\cdot \frac{s+1}{s+1} \\ (s^2 - 2s + 5)Y(s) \amp = \frac{-8}{s+1} + \frac{2s^2+10s+8}{s+1} \\ (s^2 - 2s + 5)Y(s) \amp = \frac{-8 + 2s^2+10s+8}{s+1} \\ (s^2 - 2s + 5)Y(s) \amp = \frac{2s^2+10s}{s+1} \\ Y(s) \amp = \frac{2s^2+10s}{(s+1)(s^2 - 2s + 5)} \end{align*}
    We need to do a partial fraction decomposition before we can take the inverse LT.
    \begin{align*} \frac{2s^2+10s}{(s+1)(s^2 - 2s + 5)} \amp = \frac{A}{s+1} + \frac{Bs+C}{s^2 - 2s + 5} \\ 2s^2 + 10s \amp = A(s^2 - 2s + 5) + (Bs+ C)(s+1) \\ \amp = As^2 - 2As + 5A + Bs^2 + Bs + Cs + C \\ \amp = (A + B)s^2 + (-2A + B + C)s + (5A + C) \end{align*}
    Equating the coefficients of like terms yields, we can solve fo \(\ds A \) \(\ds B \) , an \(\ds C \) .
    \begin{align*} A+B \amp = 2 \amp -2A+B+C \amp= 10 \amp 5A + C \amp= 0 \\ \amp \amp \amp \amp C \amp = -5A \\ \amp \amp \amp \amp \amp \amp -2A + B + (-5A) \amp = 10 \amp \amp \\ \amp \amp -7A + B \amp = 10 \amp \amp \\ \amp \amp B \amp = 10+7A \amp \amp \\ \amp \amp \amp \amp A + (10+7A) \amp = 2 \amp \amp \amp \amp \\ 8A+10 \amp = 2 \amp \amp \amp \amp \\ 8A \amp = -8 \amp \amp \amp \amp \\ A \amp = -1 \amp \amp \amp \amp \\ \amp \amp B \amp = 3 \amp \amp \\ \amp \amp \amp \amp C \amp = 5 \end{align*}
    Hence, we have \begin{equation} Y(s) = \frac{-1}{s+1} + \frac{3s+5}{s^2 - 2s + 5}. \label{Y_of_s} \end{equation} We need to take the inverse Laplace Transform to fin \(\ds y(t). \) We need to complete the square on the irreducible quadratic term as follows.
    \begin{align*} s^2 - 2s + 5 \amp = (s^2 - 2s) + 5 \\ \amp = (s^2 - 2s + 1) - 1 + 5 \\ \amp = (s - 1)^2 + 4 \end{align*}
    We need to transform the second term, as follows, so we can use L8 and L7 on the Laplace transform table.
    \begin{align*} \frac{3s+5}{s^2 - 2s + 5} \amp = \frac{3s+5}{(s - 1)^2 + 2^2} \\ \amp = \frac{3(s-1)+3+5}{(s - 1)^2 + 2^2} \\ \amp = \frac{3(s-1)+8}{(s - 1)^2 + 2^2} \\ \amp = \frac{3(s-1)}{(s - 1)^2 + 2^2} + \frac{8}{(s - 1)^2 + 2^2} \\ \amp = 3\cdot\frac{s-1}{(s - 1)^2 + 2^2} + \frac{4\cdot 2}{(s - 1)^2 + 2^2} \\ \amp = 3\cdot\frac{s-1}{(s - 1)^2 + 2^2} + 4\cdot\frac{2}{(s - 1)^2 + 2^2} \end{align*}
    We are now prepared to take the inverse LT of equation \ref{Y_of_s} to get the solution to the IVP.
    \begin{align*} y(t) \amp = \lap^{-1}\left\{ Y(s) \right\} \\ \amp = \lap^{-1}\left\{ \frac{-1}{s+1} + \frac{3s+5}{s^2 - 2s + 5} \right\} \\ \amp = \lap^{-1}\left\{ -1\cdot \frac{1}{s+1} + 3\cdot\frac{s-1}{(s - 1)^2 + 2^2} + 4\cdot\frac{2}{(s - 1)^2 + 2^2} \right\} \\ \amp = -1\cdot \lap^{-1}\left\{ \frac{1}{s+1}\right\} + 3\cdot\lap^{-1}\left\{\frac{s-1}{(s - 1)^2 + 2^2} \right\} + 4\cdot\lap^{-1}\left\{\frac{2}{(s - 1)^2 + 2^2} \right\} \\ \amp = -e^{-t} + 3e^t\cos(2t) + 4e^t\sin(2t) \end{align*}
