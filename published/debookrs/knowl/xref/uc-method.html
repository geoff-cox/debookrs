Method 2. Undetermined Coefficients.

The general solution to the differential equation
\begin{equation} a_n\ y^{(n)} + a_{n-1}\ y^{(n-1)} + \cdots + a_2\ y'' + a_1\ y' + a_0\ y = f(x),\tag{23} \end{equation}
has a homogeneous part, \(y_h\ \text{,}\) and particular part, \(y_p\ \text{,}\) given by
\begin{equation} y = y_h + y_p,\tag{24} \end{equation}
and can be solved through the following steps...
Step 1: Verify Method
Check that the DE is linear and has constant coefficients.
Step 2: Find \(y_h\)
Use the LHCC Method on ? to find \(y_h\text{.}\)
Step 3: Guess \(y_p\)
Guess the form of \(y_p\) based on the form of \(f(x)\text{.}\)
Step 4: Find \(y_p\)
Plug \(y_p\) into (23) and solve for the unknown coefficients.
Step 5: Give Solution
Write down the general solution and apply initial conditions.
