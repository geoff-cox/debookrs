Solving Quadratic Equations.

The solution to the quadratic equation
\begin{equation} a x^2 + b x + c = 0 \tag{16} \end{equation}
is given by the quadratic formula:
\begin{equation} x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{\color{blue} b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}\text{.}\tag{17} \end{equation}
Let
  • \(x_1\) and \(x_2\) be the two solutions from (17), and
  • \({\color{blue} \Delta = b^2 - 4ac} \ \) (value under the root in (17), i.e. discriminant).
Then we know:
  • Equation (16) can be written as
    \begin{gather*} (x - x_1)(x - x_2) = 0 \text{.} \end{gather*}
  • If \({\color{blue} \Delta > 0}\text{,}\) then \(x_1\) and \(x_2\) are different real numbers.
  • If \({\color{blue} \Delta = 0}\text{,}\) then \(x_1\) and \(x_2\) are the same real number (repeated).
  • If \({\color{blue} \Delta < 0}\text{,}\) then \(x_1\) and \(x_2\) are complex and can be written as
    \begin{equation*} x_1 = \alpha + \beta i, \quad x_2 = \alpha - \beta i\text{.} \end{equation*}
